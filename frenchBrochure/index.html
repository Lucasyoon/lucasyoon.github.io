Instructions

Preheat oven to 375 degrees. Pour flour and ¼ cup cocoa powder into a small bowl. Using an electric mixer, beat egg whites and salt in a large bowl until foamy. add ⅓ cup sugar and beat until stiff. beat egg yolks and ⅓ cup granulated sugar in a large bowl until thick, Beat in chocolate mixture until no streaks remain. Scrape batter into prepared baking sheet; smooth top (you want to have an even layer). Bake until top of cake is dry .

History

starting on 12th century, it was on Christmas Eve in many European countries to bring home a big piece of wood that would be sprinkled with salt and wine and burnt to protect their house from evil spirits.but on the 19th century, people started replacing it with a roll cake that looks like a log.

how to serve & eat

Traditional ylue logs are usually Served with only one end cut to Represent a chopped off branch. Also, sometimes people will add Powdered sugar , snowflakes , And ivy. In dinner tables, yule Logs will often be served with Espresso and other kinds of Coffie.
